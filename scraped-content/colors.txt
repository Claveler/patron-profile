Main colors

Our approach to defining colors is based on a core palette consisting of 6 colors. These colors are utilized to generate the values for all color variants palettes, which are designed to maintain accessibility while effectively conveying our brand's personality. This method is known as semantic colors.

With semantic colors, we have the flexibility to update colors easily, ensure visual consistency across our products.

Semantic meaning

Semantic is attributed to colors in our design system, with each color carrying a specific significance based on its function within the interface. The role of a color determines its usage and defines its variants, making customization and modification easier.

Naming patterns are utilized in design tools to identify variants and reference their usage, and color role. By following these naming conventions, designers can easily identify and utilize the appropriate color variants for their designs. Alias token list

Use

	

Role




Text

	

Text colors are applied to titles, paragraphs, or icons, and can be used to prioritize contents with main and primary colors. They can also indicate that contents are interactive with the action color, or that content is selected/unselected, with active and disabled colors.

When using these colors, it's essential to consider accessibility.




Background

	

The background color is typically located behind the content and used to prioritize elements with primary, subtles, and accent. It can also be used to express our brand identity or highlight elements with the brand and accent colors.




Border

	

Border colors are applied on or around background colors and used to box elements prioritized with main and primary colors. They can also be used to give specific meanings like selection, success, error, etc.
